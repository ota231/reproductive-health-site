"It is not prevention, it is protection!"
A one-stop-shop to learn everything related to birth control.
REMINDER: None of these birth control methods prevent STDs so they must be used in addition to Condoms.
Birth Control Pills
💰$0 - $50💰
What is it? The pill contains hormones that:
  1. Stops ovulation
  2. Thins uterine lining
  3. Thickens cervical mucus
Administration:Once a day at the same time every day

Side Effects
Birth Control Shots
💰$30 - $75 per shot💰
What is it?The shot contains the hormone, Progestin which stops ovulation and thickens cervical mucus
Administration:One-shot every three months administered by a medical professional

Side Effects
Patch
💰$0 - $150💰
What is it? Releases hormones that stop the ovaries from releasing eggs and thickens cervical mucus, making it difficult for sperm to enter the uterus
Administration:Apply the patch weekly anywhere on the skin except the breasts

Side Effects:
Implants
💰$0 - $1,300💰
What is it? The implant releases a hormone, progestin to stop ovulation and thicken cervical mucus to stop sperm from entering the uterus
Administration:A small tube placed inside the body (in the upper arm) by a medical professional
Benefits
Side Effects
IUD
💰$0 - $1,300💰
What is it?Releases Progestin into the body to makes the mucus on your cervix thicker blocking sperm so it can't get to an egg and stopping ovulation which means there's no egg for a sperm to fertilize.
Administration: A T-shaped plastic is inserted into the uterus by a medical professional
Benefits
Side Effects
Ring
💰$0 - $200💰
What is it?The ring contains the hormones estrogen and progestin. The hormones stop ovulation and thicken the mucus on your cervix.
Administration:You wear the ring inside your vagina, and you absorb the hormones into your body through your vaginal lining.
